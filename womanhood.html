Did You Know?

The absence of sanitary products forces many to resort to unhygienic alternatives such as coconut husk and newspaper.

Background

In Malaysia, 46% of young girls are still not fully aware of menstrual hygiene practices before their first period.

Many face embarrassment, misinformation, or lack of access to proper menstrual products and guidance, particularly in rural or underprivileged communities.

Consequences

Health Issues

Improper menstrual hygiene can lead to infections such as urinary tract infections (UTIs) and reproductive health complications.

School Absenteeism

Many girls skip school during their period due to shame, fear of leaks, or lack of access to sanitary products.

Fear and Stigma

Societal taboos perpetuate silence, making menstruation a source of shame rather than pride and understanding.
