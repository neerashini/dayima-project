Our Founder

Founded by Jia Hui, the Da Yi Ma Project is an initiative that empowers women in Malaysia through menstrual awareness and proper menstrual hygiene practices.

Our journey began with a simple yet powerful mission — to break taboos surrounding menstruation and to foster a safe, open space for conversations about womanhood.

Through community outreach, donation drives, and education programs, we aim to ensure that every girl and woman has access to safe menstrual care and knowledge. We believe that menstrual health is not a privilege, but a right.

Who Are We?

We are a collective of passionate youth advocates, educators, and changemakers under the Roots & Shoots Malaysia Award by Dr. Jane Goodall. Our project bridges education, community service, and empowerment — ensuring that conversations around menstruation are inclusive, informed, and stigma-free.

The Da Yi Ma Project works with schools, universities, and NGOs to host workshops, exhibitions, and donation drives that raise awareness on menstrual equity and hygiene practices.

Vision and Mission

Our Vision

To create a world where menstruation is celebrated, not stigmatized — where every woman and girl has access to menstrual products, education, and dignity.

Our Mission

To end period poverty in Malaysia through education, awareness, and community-led action. We strive to empower individuals with knowledge, compassion, and resources to make informed choices about menstrual health.
